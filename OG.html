Svākkhāto Bhagavatā dhammo, Sandiṭṭhiko, Akāliko, Ehipassiko, Opaneyyiko, Paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhi

Svākkhāto dhammo

The Dharma consists of ten correct dharmas that the Buddha preached perfectly, with a beginning, middle, and end, with full and clear meaning

Sandiṭṭhiko

The Dharma includes 9 Dharmas that go past the three realms that the Arahants have attained the 4 Paths - 4 Holy Results and Nibbana, then see and know for themselves with their own wisdom.

Akāliko

The Dhamma is the Four Holy Paths that give corresponding results. The Four Holy Paths have no time interval, meaning that after attaining a Path, that Result immediately gives rise to that Result after a moment of mind destruction and then birth.

Ehipassiko

The true Dharma are the nine Dharmas that transcend the three realms and belong to the True Dharma, which is very pure and clean, so everyone is dared you to witness, to truly realize, to truly attain.

Opaneyyiko

The true Dharma is the nine Dharmas that transcend the three realms. This is the Dharma that one should first direct the mind to attain in order to hope to be liberated from the suffering of birth, death, and reincarnation in the three realms and four lower species.

Paccattaṃ veditabbo viññūhi

That Dharma is the nine Dharmas that transcend the three realms that the wise and holy ones have attained, know for themselves, and enter the Holy Result of enjoying the peaceful and tranquil Nirvana.









Suppaṭipanno Bhagavato sāvakasaṃgho. Ujuppaṭipanno Bhagavato sāvakasaṃgho. Ñāyappaṭipanno Bhagavato sāvakasaṃgho. Sāmīcippaṭipanno Bhagavato sāvakasaṃgho. Yadidaṃ cattāri purisayugāni aṭṭhapurisapuggalā esa Bhagavato sāvakasaṃgho. Āhuneyyo, Pāhuneyyo, Dakkhiṇeyyo, Añjalikaraṇīyo, Anuttaraṃ puññak-khettaṃ lokassa

Suppaṭipanno

The Holy Disciples of the Buddha were those who strictly followed the Buddha's teachings.

Ujuppaṭipanno

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are those who have honestly and correctly practiced the Middle Way, without any deviation or error.

Ñāyappaṭipanno

The Holy disciples of the Buddha, are those who have followed the Noble Eightfold Path to attain Nibbana, liberating themselves from the suffering of birth and death in the cycle of rebirth in the three realms and four species.

Sāmīcippaṭipanno

Yadidaṃ cattāri purisayugāni aṭṭhapurisapuggalā esa

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are those who have practiced the correct precepts, concentration, and wisdom and are worthy of being respected, worshiped, and offered alms by living beings.

Āhuneyyo

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are worthy to receive the offerings that donors from far away bring to the Buddha, hoping to receive great rewards.

Pāhuneyyo

The Holy Disciples of the Buddha are worthy to receive the things that donors offer to distinguished guests.

Dakkhiṇeyyo

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are worthy to receive the offerings that donors with pure faith in the Triple Gem, believing in karma and its results, offer to the Buddha, hoping to receive good results for themselves and their loved ones.

Añjalikaraṇīyo

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are worthy of being respectfully bowed to and made offerings to by living beings.

Anuttaraṃ puññakkhettaṃ lokassa

The Holy disciples of the Buddha are the supreme for all living beings that is incomparable.
