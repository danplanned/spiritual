The 8 Great Hells Definition and Explanations

  1. Sañjiva Hell (The Hell of Repeated Revival)

    This is called Sañjīva because beings here die and come back to life again and again

    Sañjīva means repeated revival or reviving again

    The wardens of hell wield blazing, sharp weapons and cut the hell-beings apart. Even after being cut to death, they come back to life

  2. Kāḷasutta Hell (The Black-Line Hell)

    This is called Kāḷasutta because the wardens mark beings with black lines and then cut, slice, or chop them apart.

    After marking them with black lines, wardens will cut them with knives, axes, saws, and other tools.

    The wardens pursue fleeing beings, knock them down, mark them with black lines, then cut them apart.

  3. Saṅghāta Hell (The Crushing Hell)

    This is Saṅghāta because beings here are crushed together

    Saṅghāta means crushing or mass compression

    Here great mountains of blazing iron grind beings into powder. When being sink waist-deep into a ground of red-hot iron and are crushed by enormous iron mountains.

  4. Roruva Hell (Dhūmaroruva or Cūḷaroruva) – The Wailing Hell

    This is called Roruva because beings there cry out with great screams.

    The beings in this hell continually wail and cry in agony. The beings dwelling there cry out loud for a very long time.

    Smoke enters the nine bodily openings of the hell beings, causing unbearable suffering. It can also be called the Smoke-Wailing Hell

  5. Mahāroruva Hell (Jālaroruva) – The Great Wailing Hell

    This hell is named Mahāroruva because the cries of suffering there are even greater than those heard in Cūḷaroruva (the Lesser Wailing Hell).

    Flames enter through the nine bodily openings of the beings and burn them intensely. Their anguished cries resound pitifully throughout the hell.

  6. Tāpana Hell (Cūḷatāpana) – The Burning Hell

    The hell subjects beings to go through intense heat and burning suffering.

    Evil beings are impaled or bound upon red-hot iron stakes and burned by fire.

  7. Mahātāpana Hell (Patāpana) – The Great Burning Hell

    The beings here are burned to an extreme degree. The suffering caused by heat here is far greater than Tāpana

    Evil beings are driven up blazing mountains of iron and then hurled downward onto iron spears below by force of burning winds and flames.

  8. Avīci Hell (The Hell Without Interruption)

    In this hell, there is no break between the flames and the painful experiences that they undergo.

    The realm is the dwelling place of foolish and evil beings.


The 5 Ussada Hells Definition and Explanations

  1. Gūtha Hell (The Hell of Filth and Excrement)

    Even after being released from the Great Avīci Hell, beings whose evil kamma has not yet been exhausted are not completely freed. They continue to undergo suffering and torment in the foul Hell of Excrement (Gūtha), which lies adjacent to Avīci Hell.

  2. Kukkula Hell (The Hell of Burning Ashes)

    Although beings may escape from the Hell of Filth, if remnants of their evil kamma still remain, they are not yet fully released. They must continue to suffer in the Hell of Burning Ashes (Kukkula), which lies next to the Hell of Excrement.

  3. Simpalīvana Hell (The Forest of Silk-Cotton Thorns)

    After escaping from the Hell of Burning Ashes, beings whose evil kamma has not yet been exhausted must continue to suffer in the Forest of Thorny Silk-Cotton Trees (Simpalīvana), which lies adjacent to the Hell of Burning Ashes.

  4. Asipattavana Hell (The Forest of Sword Leaves)

    The hell-beings who still possess remaining evil kamma, though freed from the Forest of Thorny Silk-Cotton Trees, are not yet liberated from suffering. They must continue to endure torment in the Forest of Sword Leaves (Asipattavana), which lies next to the Forest of Thorny Silk-Cotton Trees.

  5. Vettaraṇī Hell (The River of Salty Water with Thorny Reeds)

    Even after escaping from the Forest of Sword Leaves, beings whose evil kamma has not yet been exhausted continue to suffer in the Vettaraṇī Hell, a river of salty water filled with thorny reeds and spikes, which lies beyond the Forest of Sword Leaves.
